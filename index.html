Hierarchical Denoising for Multi-Step Visual Reasoning

Abstract

We present HDR, a simple hierarchical denoising framework for multi-step visual reasoning in video generation. HDR combines coarse-to-fine latent planning with causal streaming: coarse levels maintain uncertain global hypotheses, and fine levels refine them into concrete visual states. This design improves reasoning consistency while keeping low-latency generation. On six tasks (maze navigation, Tower of Hanoi, one-line drawing, sliding puzzle, Sokoban, and water pouring), HDR improves success from 34.22 to 60.29 and average progress from 76.00 to 89.56 versus a streaming autoregressive diffusion baseline. During streaming, HDR runs at 0.70s per latent and is 54.2× faster than bidirectional diffusion, while also showing stronger data efficiency.
